Frequently Asked Questions About Tree Removal

How Much Does A Tree Removal Cost?

A professional arborist's assessment of the tree in question is necessary before providing an estimate for its removal.


The price of tree removal depends on several factors, including those listed below.


  • Location
  • Tree size
  • Your specifications
  • Equipment needed
  • Safety and risk concerns
  • Time and crew size


If you need a tree removed, look no further than Forrest Tree Care. We will work with you every step to ensure a hassle-free experience.

Do You Need To Get Rid Of The Tree Stump?

It's possible that an old tree stump won't be a significant issue at first, but as time goes on, it'll only become more of an eyesore and a problem.

A decaying stump will eventually become a haven for wood-destroying insects like termites and carpenter ants.

Which is Better: Grinding or Removing a Stump?

Removing a tree stump is a more laborious and disruptive process that requires heavy equipment and a lot of time. The bright side is that you can start fresh with any landscaping projects you may have.

While stump grinding saves time and effort, it does not remove the roots.

Is My Tree Dangerous Enough To Warrant Removal?

Consider many things if your tree looks like it might fall. The entire tree, including the roots, trunk, and canopy, should be evaluated to determine whether it poses a threat or is simply a natural phenomenon.

Consider the following warning signs as you examine your tree:

Is your tree leaning?

Leaning that occurs suddenly may indicate a damaged root system, which makes the tree unstable and dangerous. Our experts can also determine whether the leaning of your tree is the result of improper tree growth or the presence of disease.

Do the roots of your tree appear to be decaying or damaged?

Soaking or aerating the soil, applying mulch, and cabling the tree are all methods you can use to provide the tree with the nutrients it needs. However, the damage may be irreparable in some instances. When you hire us for tree service, our arborists will sit down with you and talk through everything so you can make educated decisions.

Is the trunk of your tree riddled with fissures, holes, and other defects?


Construction equipment, lawnmowers, storms, and fires are common causes of accidental tree damage. The tree's protective outer layer is compromised when cracks or cavities damage the bark. Water and nutrients can be dispersed through this layer. However, if the tree has more than 25% visible damage, the bark necessitates expert removal.

Has your tree's upper crown been affected by dead or drooping branches?

It's possible that the tree is unhealthy or needs some careful trimming to remove those stray branches. Our arborists will inspect the tree to determine if it’s beyond saving or if there are crown density reductions that will keep the tree healthy without posing a threat.
